Billie Holiday

Billie Holiday (AKA Eleanora Fagan - April 7, 1915 â€“ July 17, 1959), was an American jazz and swing music singer with a career spanning 26 years. Nicknamed "Lady Day" Her vocal style, strongly inspired by jazz instrumentalists, pioneered a new way of manipulating phrasing and tempo. She was known for her vocal delivery and improvisational skills. Throughout the 1930s and 1940s, Holiday had mainstream success on labels such as Columbia and Decca. She was a successful concert performer throughout the 1950s with two further sold-out shows at Carnegie Hall.
